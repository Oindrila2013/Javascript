Our Solar System

Our Solar System is a vast system dominated by the Sun, a star whose gravity holds everything else in orbit. It includes eight planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune), dwarf planets like Pluto, hundreds of moons, and countless asteroids and comets. The inner, rocky planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, while the outer planets—Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune—are giant gas or ice worlds. The system extends out to the Oort Cloud, a vast, distant region containing icy bodies, marking the gravitational boundary of the Solar System.
